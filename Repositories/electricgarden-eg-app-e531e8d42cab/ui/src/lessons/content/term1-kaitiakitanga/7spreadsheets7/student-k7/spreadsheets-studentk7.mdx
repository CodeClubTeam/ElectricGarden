##### In a spreadsheet, data is sorted into **cells** that are collected in an array of **rows** and **columns**.

##### Since each cell is at the crossing of a column and a row, we can use this to give each cell a name, like an address, to find information easily.

##### For example, this spreadsheet shows how can we label to the cell with **Māia's name** as **A2**:

<Figure>

##### The the general rule for naming a cell goes like this:

<br />

<Captioned font={'Comfortaa'} size={'25'}>
    <b>Column Letter + Row Number = Cell Name</b>
</Captioned>

##### So, we can say Māia's name is in **cell A2**, because it's in **column A** and **row 2**.

##### **Here's what the arrows mean for this example spreadsheet program:**

<Figure align={'left'}>
    <ImageWithText src={arrowno} text={'Original Order'} />
    <ImageWithText src={arrowup} text={'Progressive Order'} />
    <ImageWithText src={arrowdown} text={'Reverse Order'} />
</Figure>

-   ###### **No Arrow** = the original order that the data appeared in the spreadsheet
-   ###### **Up Arrow** = progressive order, such as from 1 up to 10, or A to Z
-   ###### **Down Arrow** = reverse order, such as from 10 down to 1, or Z to A

<br />

##### This means that you can sort data **numerically** from lowest to highest, or highest to lowest, and reset to the original input order.

##### It also means that the data will sort **alphabetically** from A to Z, or Z to A, and reset again as well.
